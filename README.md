# 8-2-Journal-Portfolio-Submission
- Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
The DriverPass project involved designing a complete system for the client, DriverPass, a company focused on helping students pass their DMV driving tests. They wanted a cloud‑based system that would allow customers to register, schedule driving lessons, access online instructional materials, take practice exams, and track their progress, while also supporting internal staff with scheduling, account management, reporting, and DMV update integration.

- What did you do particularly well?
I did particularly well in translating the client’s interview information into clear, organized requirements and system components. I effectively identified the necessary functional and nonfunctional requirements, especially around security, scheduling, user roles, and activity tracking.

- If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I were to revise one part of my work, I would improve the Gantt chart or the user interface section. The Gantt chart could be refined to better reflect the project timeline described in the interview, and the user interface section could be expanded with more detail about layout, navigation flow, and accessibility to create a more complete design.

- How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I interpreted the user’s needs by focusing on the specific problems and expectations described in the interview, such as the need for lesson scheduling, online learning tools, progress tracking, and staff‑level account management. I implemented these needs by turning them into functional requirements and system features that directly support the workflows the client described. Considering user needs is essential because a system must solve the client’s real problems and support their actual processes; otherwise, even a technically strong system will fail to meet expectations.

- How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
I approach software design by first gathering and clarifying requirements, then breaking them into functional and nonfunctional categories, and finally modeling the system using diagrams and structured documentation. In the future, I would continue using techniques such as user stories, use cases, iterative refinement, early prototyping, and UML modeling to ensure the system is well‑structured, user‑focused, and aligned with the client’s goals.
